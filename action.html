Where We Stand.

Not selling you on cannabis. Giving you honest information and letting you decide for yourself.

I use a balanced CBD/THC blend to manage chronic neuropathic atrophy and anxiety. Not chasing a high — managing a condition. There's a difference, and that difference is the whole point of this site.

The cannabis conversation in this country has two broken extremes: prohibition-era fear tactics that ignore 80+ years of suppressed research, and a commercial market that bred the brakes out of the plant and calls it progress. Neither one serves patients. Neither one serves the truth.

The traditional cannabis plant had a THC-to-CBD ratio near 14:1. Modern commercial products have pushed that past 80:1. CBD is protective. The industry removed it. That's not the plant's fault — it's a regulatory failure, and we should say so plainly.

My Position

Reschedule Federally End the Schedule I blockade that prevents the clinical trials we need.
Fund the Science Real trials. Real data. Open results.
Protect Functional Access For the millions already using cannabis therapeutically — often because the healthcare system failed them first.
Restrict Synthetic & High-Potency Products Stop products reaching kids through gas station loopholes and unregulated channels.
Regulate by Evidence, Not Stigma Policy should follow science. Not the other way around.

On Cannabis & Mental Health

The relationship is more complicated than the headlines suggest. Emerging research — including Mendelian randomization studies — points to a largely inverted relationship. Pre-existing vulnerabilities, often unaddressed because of a healthcare system with 67-day psychiatrist wait times and costs that lock out 60% of Americans, drive people toward self-medication. They arrive at a dispensary because the clinic door was closed.

That's a healthcare crisis wearing a cannabis mask.

Cannabis is not harmless. High-potency, unbalanced products carry real risks — especially for young or vulnerable users. But the answer to that isn't prohibition. It's education, honest regulation, and clinical infrastructure that actually reaches people.

The Training Gap

9% U.S. medical schools that include cannabis education in curriculum
84.9% Medical residents reporting zero clinical training on cannabinoid medicine

Patients are making decisions without clinical guidance, and providers can't fill that gap because they were never trained to. That's not a patient failure. That's a system failure.
